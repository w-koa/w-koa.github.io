# About Me as a Cellist
Willis Koa is a cellist and teacher based in Metro Detroit. He is assistant principal cellist of Battle Creek Symphony Orchestra and a member of the Lansing Symphony Orchestra. Willis frequently performs with several regional orchestras in Central and West Michigan. He has performed as a part of the West Michigan Symphony, South Bend Symphony Orchestra, Southwest Michigan Symphony Orchestra, and Holland Symphony Orchestra.

As a collaborative musician, Willis performs in a duo with cellist, Dr. Jennifer Pittman. He was a primary member of the University of Tennessee Contemporary Music Ensemble, UT Graduate Quartet/Quintet, and Western Michigan University’s contemporary music ensemble, Birds on a Wire. In addition, he has participated in chamber ensembles at several music festivals including Round Top Festival Institute, Wintergreen Summer Music Academy, and Meadowmount School of Music.

During his studies, Willis has had the opportunity to perform in various masterclasses and coachings with prominent performing artists and professors. He has performed for David Ying, Stephen Balderston, Melissa Kraut, the Doric String Quartet, and more.

An active educator, Willis instructs a private studio.  He is a member of the Suzuki Association of the Americas and plans to continue training for the benefit of his students. Previously, Willis served as graduate teaching assistant at UT, providing lessons to collegiate-level major and non-major students.

Willis was a candidate for the Doctor of Musical Arts at Michigan State University before transitioning into software development. He completed his Master’s in Cello Performance at the University of Tennessee where he was awarded a teaching assistantship and holds a Bachelor of Music in Cello Performance from Western Michigan University. His primary teachers were Suren Bagratuni, Wesley Baldwin and Bruce Uchimura.
